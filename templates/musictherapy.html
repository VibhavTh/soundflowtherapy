Music Therapy and Its Benefits

By: Vibhav Thiru

Introduction

 

Mental health challenges remain a prevalent issue today in the United States as about one in five adults suffer from a diagnosable mental illness in a given year. These issues can affect people from all walks of life, cutting across age, gender, and socioeconomic boundaries. The fast-paced, modern lifestyle, coupled with external stressors and societal pressures, has contributed to the rise of mental health challenges. Traditional therapeutic approaches, while valuable, may not fully address the diverse needs of individuals navigating the complex terrain of mental health disorders. In response to this growing demand for holistic interventions, music therapy has emerged as a dynamic and inclusive modality. Although music has been a universal form of expression and therapy for centuries and is not a breakthrough in therapy, it has gained recognition in recent years as a structured and evidence-based form of treatment for individuals who may face mental health challenges. With the use of technology, music therapy can act as a powerful and effective form of therapy that has psychological and social benefits to its patients.

 

 

Background

 

Music therapy is an adaptable and therapeutic approach that uses music to address various physical, emotional, and cognitive needs. It is particularly effective in addressing anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder, offering a safe and non-invasive means of expression, communication, and healing. Music is used as a form of expression for patients that can improve overall well-being and quality of life. This therapy can be used in a wide range of demographics, from toddlers to the elderly, but it is tailored to fit each person’s unique situation(CloudCover Media, 2016). The flexibility of music therapy also allows it to be used in diverse treatment settings, such as hospitals, schools, rehabilitation centers, and private practices. It also aligns with the growing recognition of holistic and complementary approaches in mental health care in the U.S. The positive

impact of music therapy on mental health is currently being recorded in ongoing research by professional associations like the American Music Therapy Association.

 

 

Neurological Effects

 

Through its neurological effects and role in stress reduction, music therapy can be seen as providing psychological benefits that can have a profound impact on a person’s mental and emotional health. From a young age, human brains can identify and process music. It acts as a form of sensory stimuli that can activate more parts of the brain than other sensory stimulants and offer deep brain stimulation(Xu, 2023). The different areas of the brain responsible for processing emotions, memories, and language are stimulated by music. When music is registered by the brain, it triggers "pleasure neurotransmitters”, such as dopamine, that make you feel happy. Our ears pick up the vibrations of music sounds as they travel. The inner ear then produces electrical signals from these vibrations. Following that, neurons send these signals to particular regions of the brain’s cerebral cortex. The various components of the signals (like the pitch, rhythm, and tone) are then detected by specific brain regions(BetterSleep, 2022). Its ability to stimulate multiple areas of the brain allows it to act as a great tool for various psychological conditions, including disorders of consciousness.

 

 

Brain Disorders

 

Neurologic Music Therapy(NMT) is a specific branch of music therapy that aids brain disorders and many other problems associated with the brain such as traumatic brain injury, stroke, autism, Alzheimer’s, and Parkinson’s. Studies of people with memory disorders, such as Alzheimer’s disease, suggest that neuronal memory traces built through music are deeply ingrained and more resilient to neurodegenerative influences(Stack, 2014). Additionally, rhythmic auditory stimulation provided by music can help individuals with Alzheimer's improve motor skills and coordination. The multi-sensory nature of music engages various parts of the brain, compensating for cognitive decline and promoting a sense of joy and connection. 

 

 

Stress Reduction

 

Alongside its neurological effects, music therapy plays a major role in reducing stress and inducing relaxation. The tempo of a song can influence the pulse of neural signals in the brain, and these changes can directly affect how fast the heart beats and how fast we breathe(Moawad, 2021). Slow-tempo music, around 60 beats per minute, can make the brain synchronize with the beat, causing the same alpha brain waves that are present when we are relaxed and conscious(Moawad, 2021).  Music can influence the mood of children and can be used to reduce stress and improve the sense of well-being in the following ways: Distraction, Mindfulness, and Creative Expression. Music demands their attention, it acts as a distraction while also helping them explore their emotions. It also keeps them focused and prevents their mind from wandering to negative thoughts or worries.

 

Music can also activate the body’s natural relaxation response, leading to the relaxation of tense muscles that loosen your body and autonomically relax your mind as well. Another benefit of listening to music for stress management is increased physical relaxation and improved cardiovascular health. Studies have shown that listening to soothing music can decrease blood pressure, heart rate, and anxiety levels(Bugos, 2023). This can lead to a state of physical relaxation and improved overall cardiovascular health. Additionally, music has been found to reduce the release of stress hormones like cortisol, decreasing stress and tension in the body. Also, research has shown that listening to relaxing music before bed can help induce a state of relaxation and reduce racing thoughts, leading to a more restful night’s sleep. In addition, music can also serve as a form of white noise, blocking out disruptive sounds and promoting better sleep. By reducing feelings of stress and anxiety, individuals are able to approach tasks with a clearer and more positive mindset, resulting in greater productivity.

 

 

Social Benefits 

 

Outside of psychological benefits, this form of therapy can also be used to positively impact social well-being by acting as a conduit of emotional expression, fostering improved communications and social connections. In therapeutic settings, individuals from diverse backgrounds come together, engaging in music-related activities that transcend cultural, social, and personal differences. The shared experience of creating music enhances a feeling of unity, breaking down barriers that may exist in traditional communication(Suttie, 2015). The collaborative nature of music therapy sessions promotes mutual understanding, acceptance, and a shared sense of purpose within the group.

 

The non-verbal nature of musical expression enables participants to communicate complex emotions that may be challenging to express verbally. This aspect of the therapy is particularly beneficial for individuals who struggle with conventional communication methods or find it difficult to articulate their emotions. Whether through playing instruments, singing, or engaging in other musical activities, individuals can find a medium to convey their innermost thoughts and feelings, leading to enriched communication with others(Rumsey, 2023). This emotional release contributes to a cathartic experience, promoting mental well-being and allowing participants to explore and process their emotions in a supportive space. These therapy sessions can help people practice social skills that they can apply in their real lives(Jb Music Therapy, 2017). The sessions are usually in a group environment, which leads to a better sense of community. By engaging in music-related activities together, individuals from diverse backgrounds can find common ground, breaking down barriers and building connections through emotional expression. They can form a strong sense of unity, acceptance, and mutual understanding within the therapeutic setting.

 

 

Conclusion

 

In conclusion, the landscape of mental health care is evolving, and music therapy has positioned itself as a dynamic and evidence-backed intervention to address the prevalent challenges faced by individuals in the United States. With its inherent adaptability, neurological benefits, and stress-reducing properties, music therapy presents a holistic and promising avenue for promoting overall well-being. The specialized field of Neurologic Music Therapy (NMT) demonstrates encouraging outcomes, particularly in addressing brain-related disorders such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. Beyond individual healing, the social dimensions of music therapy, fostering communication and community in therapeutic settings, underscore its potential for building meaningful connections among diverse groups. As society embraces a more comprehensive approach to mental health, music therapy, with its universal language and technological accessibility, emerges as a valuable and impactful tool in promoting healing and self-discovery.
